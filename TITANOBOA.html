Titanoboa

Scientific Name: Titanoboa cerrejonensis

Titanoboa was the largest snake ever known, growing up to 42 feet (12.8 meters) and weighing over a ton. It lived approximately 60 million years ago in what is now Colombia, thriving in hot, humid rainforests after the extinction of the dinosaurs.

Quick Facts

Habitat: Tropical rainforests (Colombia)
Extinction: ~58-60 million years ago
Length: Over 40 feet (12 meters)
Diet: Crocodiles, turtles, large fish
